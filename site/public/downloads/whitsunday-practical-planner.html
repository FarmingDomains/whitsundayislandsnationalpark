Whitsunday Islands Practical Planner

Boat access · Whitehaven + Hill Inlet · tides · camping vs day trip · stinger & weather filters · self-sufficient packing · Ngaro respect · official links

Print this page

Verify everything officially. Camping fees, track status, boat access, marine rules and weather windows change. Use Queensland Parks (parks.qld.gov.au/parks/whitsunday-islands), GBRMPA Whitsundays access pages, BOM tides/marine forecasts, and your operator. This is an independent unofficial guide — not affiliated with QPWS, GBRMPA, Ngaro Traditional Owners, or any government agency.

1. Before you leave Airlie Beach

  • ☐ Boat path chosen: day tour · camping transfer · private / multi-day sail
  • ☐ Tour / transfer booking confirmed + offline screenshot / QR
  • ☐ Weather buffer day if Whitehaven is non-negotiable (wind cancels boats)
  • ☐ Marine forecast + BOM tide tables checked (Hill Inlet swirl is tide-dependent)
  • ☐ Park alerts checked: https://parks.qld.gov.au/park-alerts
  • ☐ Dry bag packed; phone waterproofed
  • ☐ Stinger plan if swimming (typically Nov–May — suit or skip)
  • ☐ Someone on the mainland knows your boat times / camp plan

2. Boat-access decision hierarchy

Day tour (volume path)

  • Best for first-timers & mixed groups
  • Fixed window; shared beach time
  • Confirm Hill Inlet inclusion
  • Read cancellation policy

Camping transfer (immersion)

  • QPWS permit + displayed tag
  • Pack water in (no reliable taps)
  • Stove only — no campfires
  • Both-way transfer windows fixed

Private / bareboat / multi-day sail

  • Skills + GBRMPA zoning required
  • Public moorings / anchor rules
  • Weather windows stricter
  • Higher cost, higher freedom

Skip / defer if…

  • High winds / cancel SMS
  • No weather buffer on flights
  • Camping gear incomplete
  • Seasickness + rough forecast

3. Icon pair: Whitehaven + Hill Inlet

  1. Confirm product includes Hill Inlet lookout (not every Whitehaven trip does).
  2. Check tides — mid-to-high often delivers the classic swirl; low tide still beautiful, different look.
  3. Closed-toe shoes for lookout (~1.3–1.4 km return, moderate, ~40 min walking — verify officially).
  4. Write down muster / pick-up time; do not miss the boat for “one more photo”.
  5. Beach time: sun protection + hydration; swim only if conditions and stinger guidance allow.

4. Day-trip vs overnight camping

Day trip honesty

  • Shared icon beach in peak season
  • Clock set by the boat
  • Lower gear load
  • Still world-class silica sand

Overnight camping

  • Book: book.parks.qld.gov.au / parks.qld.gov.au camping
  • Fees apply — re-check live $
  • Tag displayed at site
  • Quieter dawn / dusk hours
  • ☐ Water plan: often 5 L+ per person per day (no reliable fresh water on most sites — verify official advice)
  • ☐ Stove + fuel (no campfires)
  • ☐ Waste pack-out bags
  • ☐ Pest-free / biosecurity gear check (boats, clothes, kit)
  • ☐ Transfer both ways confirmed

5. Stinger, sun & marine safety

  • ☐ Stinger season awareness (typically November–May)
  • ☐ Full-body suit / lycra if snorkelling in season (or as operator requires)
  • ☐ Reef-safe high-SPF sunscreen + UPF shirt + hat with cord
  • ☐ Polarised sunglasses (glare off silica + water)
  • ☐ Dry bag + waterproof phone pouch
  • ☐ Seasickness plan if needed
  • ☐ Reef etiquette: no standing on coral; no taking shells/coral
  • ☐ GBRMPA zoning if you skipper yourself
  • ☐ No recreational drones without proper authorisation

6. Ngaro cultural respect

  • ☐ Stay on marked tracks at cultural sites
  • ☐ Do not touch rock art
  • ☐ No restricted / ceremony photography
  • ☐ Do not invent or retell sacred stories
  • ☐ Prefer operators that respect cultural and marine park protocols

7. Packing checklist (tropical boat + island)

  • ☐ Dry bag (10–20 L)
  • ☐ Waterproof phone case
  • ☐ UPF long-sleeve / rash shirt
  • ☐ Wide-brim hat with cord
  • ☐ Closed-toe shoes (lookout + landings)
  • ☐ Stinger suit / lycra (seasonal)
  • ☐ Water + snacks (even if lunch included)
  • ☐ Reef-safe sunscreen
  • ☐ Polarised sunglasses
  • ☐ Headlamp (if camping)
  • ☐ Collapsible water jugs (if camping)
  • ☐ Stove + fuel (if camping)
