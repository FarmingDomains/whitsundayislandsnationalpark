Whitsunday Islands Practical Planner

Unofficial checklist for Whitehaven Beach, Hill Inlet, boat access and camping. Always verify with Queensland Parks and your operator before you go.

Not affiliated with QPWS, GBRMPA, Ngaro Traditional Owners, or any government agency. Fees, track status and marine conditions change.

1. Boat-access decision

  • □ Day tour from Airlie / Shute Harbour (volume path)
  • □ Camping transfer + QPWS permit (immersion path)
  • □ Private vessel / bareboat / multi-day sail (freedom path — skills required)
  • □ Weather buffer day booked if Whitehaven is non-negotiable

2. Icon pair: Whitehaven + Hill Inlet

  • □ Product inclusions checked (does it include Hill Inlet lookout?)
  • □ BOM tide tables checked (swirl is tide-dependent)
  • □ Closed-toe shoes for ~1.3 km return moderate lookout track
  • □ Muster / pick-up times written down

3. Camping (if overnight)

  • □ QPWS camping booking confirmed + tag display plan
  • □ Water packed (plan generously; no reliable fresh water on most sites)
  • □ Stove + fuel (no campfires)
  • □ Waste pack-out bags
  • □ Pest-free / biosecurity gear check
  • □ Transfer windows confirmed both ways

4. Stinger, sun & marine safety

  • □ Stinger season awareness (typically Nov–May) — suit or skip swim
  • □ Reef-safe sunscreen + UPF clothing + hat with cord
  • □ Dry bag + waterproof phone pouch
  • □ Seasickness plan if needed
  • □ Reef etiquette: no standing on coral, no taking shells/coral

5. Cultural respect

  • □ Stay on marked tracks at cultural sites
  • □ No restricted rock-art photography
  • □ Support operators that respect Ngaro protocols and marine rules
